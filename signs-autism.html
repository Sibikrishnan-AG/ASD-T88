Early indicators in young children

Early identification of autism symptoms in young children is crucial for timely intervention and support. While common signs like hyperactivity and aggression shown in behavior, there are other factors that are often overlooked due to the challenges to catching them. Here are some tips families can look out for:

• Avoiding eye contact • Difficulties in verbally expressing cohesive thoughts (e.g. unable to form sentences, delayed responses) • Sensory sensitivities • Repeat phrases or words they hear (echolalia) instead of generating original speech • Fixated focus on specific interests, such as topics, objects, or activities Disclaimer:
This information is for educational purposes only. If you're unsure whether your child has ASD (autism), please consult a doctor or specialist for an accurate diagnosis and guidance.

Next steps families can take

Autism presents with varying traits and severity from individual to individual, so it's crucial for parents to collaborate closely with medical professionals to develop a personalized care plan. Working together with doctors, and medical specialists, parents can explore a variety of interventions, such as speech and occupational therapy and social skills training.

Understanding a child's specific sensory triggers is vital in managing behavioral responses and ensuring the child's comfort in daily activities. Identifying these triggers allows parents and professionals to create environments that minimize sensory overload and help the child better regulate emotions and reactions.

With early intervention, children can make significant progress in their development over time.
