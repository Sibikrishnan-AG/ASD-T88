Early indicators in young children

Early identification of ADHD symptoms in young children is crucial for timely intervention and support. While common signs like hyperactivity and unusual behavior are typical signs of ADHD, there are other factors that are often overlooked due to the challenges to catching them. Here are some tips families can look out for:

• Check for child's conditions for a minimum of 6 months. Younger children tend to have outgoing and active personality, to avoid wrong diagnosis it is recommended to observe their conditions throughly during those period. Look out for persistent signs in hyperactivity, unusual behaviours and short attention span.

• Use these three key indicators that can help with your diagnosis: (1) How does the child plan and approach interactions? (2) What is the activity level in executing certain actions? (3) How attentive and focus is the child when engaged in these interactions?

• For infants, look out for delays in speech (language), motor, emotional patterns

• Regression in academic performance, whereby the child struggles to keep up with their peers

• Impulsive traits, whereby the child tend to act before thinking or failure to follow directions

Next steps families can take

ADHD variants and severity differ from individual to individual, so it’s important for parents to work closely with doctors to develop a tailored treatment plan and therapy. By collaborating with healthcare professionals, parents can explore options such as medication, behavioral therapy, and lifestyle changes, which can help manage symptoms and potentially reduce their severity over time. Early intervention and consistent support can significantly improve a child’s ability to cope with ADHD.
